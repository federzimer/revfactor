I see different reasons why people get into the short-term rentals. It could be the lifestyle, getting out of their 9 to 5, or it could be starting a side hustle, building generational wealth, whatever it is. It doesn't matter the reason, there's one thing you need to keep in mind if you want to be successful. This is a people's business. This is not just another real estate investment. So, if you don't see the ROI on adding extra amenities, making the stay more comfortable, and having all the patience and emotional intelligence to deal with the different problems that are going to happen throughout the stay, you may need to either find another thing to do or hire someone that can help you with that part of dealing with people. At the end of the day, people are choosing to spend time at your property, and time is the most valuable thing that a person has.
