Here's a mindset tip that's going to make you a better host with a better listing. Assume no guest read through your listing description or go through all of your photos. Think about the shop process on Airbnb. Guest have a bunch of options, most of the times people book because the property match their required boxes. This could be price, design, location, amenities, pet policy or a combination. So, to get more bookings, make sure that your first five pictures are different areas of your listing featuring the best amenities. Most people coming across your listing won't pass the first two or two or three pictures. Make sure the key points that make your property attractive are at the top of the description, usually with bullet points to make it easy for people to read. Now, if you have a specific policy or any bottleneck that you see during your stays, communicate this at the moment of booking. For example, to avoid having an uncomfortable conversation at check-in because someone brought a pet and didn't know they had to pay, let the guest know at the time of booking that this is a pet-friendly property and tell them you know if they plan to bring a pet, because a lot of people are going to come across your listing as a pet-friendly property and they do not know or will not guess they have to pay to bring a pet. So, avoid any people assuming, communicate everything up front and be a better host.
