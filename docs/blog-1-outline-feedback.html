Top-line decision

This pillar should not ship in isolation. Three things have to ship alongside it for it to do its job:

  1. A companion listicle — "Best STR Revenue Management Companies 2026" — to capture the ~60 vendor-discovery prompts in Peec that this pillar can't (the pillar is a definitional page, not a buyer-comparison page).
  2. A decision on the cannibalization triangle. Three URLs targeting the same intent right now: existing live /blog/dynamic-pricing-str-beginners-guide/ (1,126 words, indexed) + this new pillar + GetCito's planned "Dynamic Pricing vs Revenue Management: The Difference Nobody Explains" (Phase 2 item). Pick one URL to own the head term; the other two link UP to it.
  3. Brand disambiguation vs Refactor.ai — Avinash flagged this on the 4/22 call but it's not in any of the four GetCito strategy docs. Without llms.txt entity statement + Wikidata + schema sameAs locks, the pillar's AEO citations get split between revfactor.io and refactor.ai.

Top-line strategic call (refined)

This outline is directionally strong on positioning and voice. Three issues need a decision before GetCito drafts:

  1. Primary keyword has 0 measurable search volume. Ahrefs returns vol = 0 / KD = null / TP = null for "revenue management for short-term rentals" and "str pricing strategy." The actual traffic prize is "dynamic pricing airbnb" (vol 200, KD 10, TP 1,400) and "airbnb pricing strategy" (vol 250, KD 5, TP 10). This page should be built as an AEO/GEO citation play first, Google traffic play second — the secondary keywords need to bridge into the dynamic-pricing semantic cluster so the URL can also rank on the high-traffic phrase.
  2. Cannibalization triangle (see banner above). My recommendation: keep the existing /blog/dynamic-pricing-str-beginners-guide/ post but trim to ~600 words and add a "Read the full guide" callout linking up to this pillar; merge the planned "Dynamic Pricing vs Revenue Management" post into Section 4 of this pillar instead of standing it up alone.
  3. RevFactor brand has 0% Peec share-of-voice across 75 tracked AI prompts. Competitors dominate: PriceLabs 20% SOV, Vrbo 12%, Wheelhouse 11%, AirDNA 8%, Beyond Pricing 7%. ~80% of those Peec prompts are vendor-discovery intent ("who can help me…", "what companies…", "what services…") — a pillar guide will not capture those. Without a companion listicle, this pillar will earn citations on the ~10 educational prompts and miss the other ~60.

What to keep: the airline yield-management origin story, the perishable-inventory frame, the worked ADR-vs-RevPAR example, and the Tactical Playbook section. Those are the strongest assets in the doc and align tightly with Federico's persona.
