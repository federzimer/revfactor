## 1. VOICE & CADENCE

### Sentence Structure Signature
Federico uses a **conversational staircase**:
- Short punchy opener (3–7 words)
- Medium explanatory build (15–25 words)
- Longer actionable/reflective close (25–35 words)

**Example rhythm:**
> "Here's the thing. If you're launching a new listing on Airbnb and you want to rank it as high as possible, you need to think about three things. Number one, how the algorithm works — and the algorithm is based on interest because Airbnb makes money out of every transaction."

### Opening Hook Formulas (5 patterns)
1. **"Let me show you [specific thing]"** — direct demonstration (40+ uses)
2. **"What would I do differently if I managed this property?"** — critical audit frame (8+ uses)
3. **"Today/Yesterday + specific incident"** — story-driven entry (15+ uses)
4. **"If you'd asked me one thing..."** — contrarian POV signal
5. **"Have you ever..."** — empathy opener

### Closing Patterns
- Open questions to audience ("Tell me, are you interested in...?")
- Specific action statements ("Just check the website and we can schedule a call.")
- Principle restatement ("The important thing here is to understand the math.")
- Never a hard sell.

### Pacing Markers
- "Um," "uh," "so" = natural transitions, not filler
- Heavy use of lists: "Number one... the other way is..."
- Rhetorical questions mid-explanation ("right?")
- "So, again, [restatement]" for emphasis

## 4. TEACHING STYLE

### 5-Step Teaching Arc
1. **Name the problem** — "The number one mistake I see..."
2. **Explain the principle** — "Airbnb makes money out of transactions. If people aren't booking, they don't show you."
3. **Show a specific example** — Real property walkthrough, screenshot, or personal story
4. **Give the actionable step** — "Go to your listing, click edit, check every box..."
5. **Circle back to principle** — "So, again, you want to be as relevant as possible for as many searches."

### Data vs Stories vs Frameworks
- **Data-driven but not cold** — "I spoke with Airbnb today and they told me..."
- **Story-driven but not fluffy** — "Story time about when I almost lost my Airbnb account"
- **Framework-first** — principle before procedure, always
